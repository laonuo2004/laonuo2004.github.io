Hello! I'm Yilong Zuo, a third-year undergraduate in Computer Science at [Beijing Institute of Technology](https://www.bit.edu.cn/), advised by [Prof. Ronghua Li](https://ronghuali.github.io/).

## Research Interests

My research interests lie in **multimodal learning**, **graph learning**, and **large language models (LLMs)**, with a current focus on how graph structures interact with multimodal information.

I care about problems that are clearly defined and genuinely worth studying. In general, I prefer starting from concrete questions and building methodologies from there.

## Current Work

My recent work is **MAGE-RAG**, a project on multimodal RAG for long-document question answering.

The project started from a simple problem I kept running into: when a document is long and contains text, figures, tables, and layout clues, it is hard to decide what evidence should be retrieved and how it should be organized before sending it to a multimodal model.

In MAGE-RAG, I try to use graph structures to connect different pieces of document evidence, so the system can retrieve and assemble information in a more adaptive way. This work has been released on arXiv and is currently under review at ICDE 2027.
